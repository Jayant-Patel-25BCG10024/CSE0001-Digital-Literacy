### 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist for College Students

##### **Essential Prevention Tips for the Indian Digital Landscape**



**Follow these 8 actionable steps to protect your identity, data, and finances.**



1. **UPI Rule #1 (Receive vs. Send):** Never enter your UPI PIN to \*receive\* money. A PIN is only required   to \*send\* money or check your balance.
2. **Verify UPI IDs:** Before clicking 'Pay' on apps like GPay or PhonePe, double-check the verified name of   the recipient to ensure it matches the person you intend to pay.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your VIT student email, WhatsApp, and Instagram. This adds a second layer of security beyond just your password.
4. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Never log into your bank portal or VITyarthi account using free public Wi-Fi (like at railway stations or cafes) without a trusted VPN.
5. **Check for 'HTTPS':** Before entering credentials on any website, ensure the URL starts with `https://` and has a padlock icon in the address bar.
6. **Screen Sharing Caution:** Never share your screen via apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer with unknown "customer care" executives; they can see your OTPs in real-time.
7. **Social Media Privacy:** Set your Instagram/Facebook profiles to 'Private' and periodically audit your 'Followers' list to remove unknown accounts.
8. **App Permissions Audit:** Regularly check which apps have access to your Microphone, Camera, and Contacts in your phone settings—revoke access for apps that don't need them.







###### **How to Report Cybercrime in India**



If you or a peer are victims of a scam, time is critical.

* **National Cyber Crime Portal:** \[https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call immediately to freeze fraudulent transactions)
