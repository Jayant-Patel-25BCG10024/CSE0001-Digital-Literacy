##### \# Responsible Social Media Checklist for College Students



As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have compiled this list to help my peers maintain a positive, safe, and professional digital footprint.



###### DO (Best Practices)



* **Audit your privacy settings** regularly to control who sees your personal posts.  
* **Use "The Billboard Test":** Only post what you'd be comfortable seeing on a giant billboard. 
* **Network with industry professionals** and alumni on platforms like LinkedIn.
* **Give credit where it's due** when sharing others' photos, articles, or creative work. 
* **Fact-check information** before hitting "Share" to prevent the spread of misinformation. 



###### DON'T (Risks to Avoid)



* **Don't post illegal or policy-violating content** (e.g., underage drinking or exam leaks). 
* **Don't engage with trolls or cyberbullies.** Block and report instead of escalating.
* **Don't share sensitive PII** (Personally Identifiable Information) like your home address or OTPs.
* **Don't vent about professors or your university** publicly; it can have long-term consequences.
* **Don't post when you are angry or emotional.** Take 24 hours to cool down first.





Pro-Tips for Success

1\. **Google Yourself:** Search your name in an Incognito window to see what a recruiter sees.

2\. **Link Your Work:** Use your "Link in Bio" to point to your GitHub or personal portfolio.

3\. **Be Authentic, Not Perfect:** It's okay to show your personality, as long as it's respectful!
